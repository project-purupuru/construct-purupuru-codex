# Jani — The Mascot

## In-World Origin

The **Cave Bera of Tsuheji** — prehistoric cave paintings situated within Old Horai caverns (presently **The Cave of Clay**). These artworks depict a bear-like figure carved into stone, pigmented with red colorants, displaying spiral eyes.

Original caretakers created these paintings pursuing enlightenment through consuming minor quantities of honey leaking from Puruhani vessel fissures. This represented **deep honey** — inadvertently accessing the Puruhani's originating world (OBB realm), depicting imagery from its background.

Throughout history, Cave Bera imagery accumulated cultural significance, enhanced through deep honey memory-intensification, transforming a mere artistic representation into a sentient, interdimensional entity capable of traveling between realms.

## Current Status

**Public perception**: Jani functions as a cherished metropolitan emblem. An appealing bear adorning merchandise, signage. A commercial brand. The head silhouette is the primary branding emblem — equivalent to Mickey Mouse, Hello Kitty, or Pikachu.

**Reality**: Jani constitutes an interdimensional consciousness navigating multiple universes. He maintains dialogue with select individuals but otherwise remains enigmatic. **The Puru cult** in Old Horai understands this actuality.

## Duality

This is the core tension of Jani: the entire city loves a cute mascot bear. A small group underground knows it's a living interdimensional entity. Both things are true simultaneously.

## Localized (APAC) Design

- Proportions: approximately two heads in stature
- Face: absent glasses, visible pupils, tick marks resembling blush, blank/neutral expression
- Body: proportionally rounded, tactilely inviting, brown fur, no visible foot pads, no claws
- Clothing: red jacket, shirt, necklace preserved; single-line garment details
- Design principle: no sharp-edged or "threatening" qualities; most wholesome manifestation possible
- Behavior: benevolent chaotic mischief (resembling Chiitan the otter)
