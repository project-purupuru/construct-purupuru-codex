## 1. The World of Tsuheji

### Overview

**Tsuheji** (土和吉) is the fictional continent where everything in Project Purupuru takes place. The name is derived from tsuchi (土, soil), he/wa (和, harmony/peace), and ji (吉, lucky).

### World Constants

- **Aesthetic**: Ghibli-inspired. Warm, painterly, inviting.
- **Tone**: Positive, wholesome, low-conflict. Social complexities and conflict exist in this world but are not the focus of what we show. This is wholesome escapism.
- **Magic System**: All magic and whimsy is rooted in **honey**. There are sentient honey creatures (Puruhani), honeycomb is architecturally and culturally important, and bees are everywhere. No dragons, unicorns, or traditional fantasy creatures.
- **Henlo**: The primary cultural constant and cornerstone of this world. Henlo is a greeting, a lifestyle, an ethos, and for some, a belief system followed with deep devotion. It is the one unifying thread that ties everything together.
- **Technology Level**: Daily life is not dramatically different from ours. Society is slightly more utopian with a strong emphasis on sustainability and ecological living.

### Geography

Tsuheji is a continent with one main city (**Horai**) and smaller settlements connected by an extensive train network. The broader geography includes mountains, coastline, and multiple regions visible on the continental map. Details of settlements beyond Horai are intentionally undeveloped.

## 2. Horai — The Main City

### Overview

**Horai** (蜂来, "where bees gather" — also echoing 蓬莱, the mythical paradise of East Asian legend) is the main city on Tsuheji. Known for its history as a gathering place of ideas and its dedication to ecology. A paradise of green and gold.

### Structure

- **Sora Tower**: Central landmark. A massive self-sustaining architectural and ecological marvel that serves as the capital of the city. Beautiful gardens line many tiers, great vines hang down for many stories where residents harvest fresh produce from their balconies. Powered by vast solar arrays. The **Sky-eyes Dome** observatory sits at its peak.
- **Honeycomb Districts**: Neighborhoods built in the same honeycomb shape as the bees that produced the honey they treasure. This structure persisted as the city grew, creating distinct districts and even influencing the architecture of skyscrapers.
- **Train Systems**: Elaborate networks run through Old Horai underground and the surface, connecting even the furthest reaches of the city and the broader continent. The central hub is **Musubi Station** (結び, connection/tying together).
- **Ship of Theseus Architecture**: Old buildings are NEVER demolished. Conservation and preservation are pushed to the absolute limit. Historical importance and tradition come before necessity. Buildings are preserved as intensely as possible, or renovated so many times they take on an unrecognizable form. A wooden shrine gets rebuilt in steel but keeps its exact original form. Wood-and-tile houses stand alongside vast concrete apartment complexes.

## 3. Old Horai — The Underground

### Overview

The ancient original city, buried under centuries of Horai building upward. Not hidden or forbidden — just irrelevant to most modern surface residents. The train goes there, the stops exist, but surface Horai is vibrant and modern and *up*, so most people never go *down*. Like how most New Yorkers have never been to the Cloisters.

### The Puru Cult

A monastic community that never got the memo that the world moved on. They kept the old honey rituals alive, maintained the Puruhani vessels, and tended the underground shrines for centuries while everyone above forgot. They are deeply sincere, a bit eccentric, and the only people who weren't surprised when the Puruhani woke up. They are vindicated now — the weirdos were right all along.

They know the truth about Jani — that the mascot is a living, interdimensional being, not just branding.

### Character

Old Horai is a real, living neighborhood — not ruins with holdouts. The community developed differently because they're underground. They have their own shops, their own food (mushrooms, root vegetables, cave honey from deep-dwelling bees), their own aesthetic. Bioluminescent moss lighting instead of electric. Ceramics everywhere, continuing the old traditions.

### The Deep Honey

The bees in Old Horai are different. Older, stranger. They produce honey that the surface apiaries don't have. Consuming it can give vivid dreams or visions — this is how the original cave painters saw Jani's world and depicted the Cave Bera. Not dangerous, but *weird*. This is the mystical element of Tsuheji without breaking the wholesome tone.

### Visual Palette

Deep amber, moss green, clay red, bioluminescent blues and greens. Completely different from the bright green-and-gold surface.

## 4. The Puruhani

### What They Are

Sentient honey creatures created by the **Ooga Booga Beras (OBB)** from honey. The OBB attempted to create a new generation of bears from honey but encountered difficulties — the creatures lacked proper containment vessels. They were placed into makeshift clay pots and buried until the OBB could devise a solution.

The Puruhani transitioned from the OBB world into Tsuheji, materializing in a field of pots. Local inhabitants discovered them and embraced them after witnessing their capacity to motivate others. They assumed understated leadership positions, steering early Tsuheji culture toward virtue and harmony.

### The Hibernation

Believing their mission complete, the Puruhani entered profound hibernation. As Horai expanded upward over centuries, these creatures were physically interred beneath the growing city. In contemporary times, they were excavated at **The Unearthing** on Horai's eastern periphery.

### Design

All Puruhani share identical creature characteristics — soft, golden, honey-toned blobs differentiated primarily by subtle expression and posture. Each resides in a colored clay pot aligned with their Wuxing element, bearing a Henlo Bera sticker:

| Puruhani Trait | Pot Color | Wuxing Element | Bera Sticker |
|---------------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Happy | Green | Wood (木) | Panda |
| Exhausted | Yellow | Earth (土) | Brown Bear |
| Nefarious | Red | Fire (火) | Black Bear |
| Loving | Purple | Metal (金) | Polar Bear |
| Overwhelmed | Blue | Water (水) | Red Panda |

The pots feature lids or caps. Puruhani characteristically display sleepy demeanor — draping over pot edges. Their presentation conveys "recently awakened from centuries of hibernation."

### Relationship with Caretakers

All Puruhani/Caretaker pairings are **mutualistic**. Interdependence defines their bond. The relationship consistently represents mutual benefit and development.

## 5. KIZUNA — First Generation Characters

### Overview

**KIZUNA** (絆 = bond) represents the inaugural character cohort. Five young women ("Caretakers"), each matched with a Puruhani they discovered following the creatures' modern-era excavation.

Subsequent generations planned: **MIRAI** (based on five tastes) then **TENSEI**. Both remain in preliminary developmental stages.

### Characters

#### Kaori (H — Hopeful / Happy — Wood — Benevolence)
*Name meaning: fragrance, evokes nature/beauty.*

Recognized for her optimistic perspective and vibrant demeanor, Kaori imparts warmth to those around her. An aspiring gardener dedicating leisure time nurturing her community garden plot. Despite substantial effort, yields remain modest. Yet she persists, aspiring to contribute flourishing produce.

**Pair dynamic**: The most harmonious partnership. Happy supplies youthful energy; Hopeful prevents Happy from losing composure during adversity. Their unwavering commitment to spreading joy generates benevolence.

#### Nemu (E — Empty / Exhausted — Earth — Fidelity)
*Name meaning: from nemui, JP word for sleepy.*

Excessively gentle and withdrawn. A blank slate whose distinguishing characteristic involves favoring spacious, comfortable garments. Her Puru's unrelenting exhausted condition sparked protective concern, motivating her toward helping.

**Pair dynamic**: Remarkably functional despite appearance. Exhausted sustains Empty with faint post-exertion enthusiasm; Empty furnishes the companionable silence needed. Their productivity despite constant depletion demonstrates Fidelity.

#### Akane (N — Naughty / Nefarious — Fire — Propriety)
*Name meaning: JP word for deep red.*

Fierce independence and mischievous temperament. The group's boldest member. Urban exploration captivates her — nighttime rooftop scaling and infiltrating abandoned structures. Her Puru's discovery intensified these inclinations.

**Pair dynamic**: Dangerously compatible. Naughty provides Nefarious with increasingly insidious suggestions while Nefarious drives Naughty toward execution. While not actively embodying Propriety, their exploits remind society what behavioral patterns lack utility.

#### Eun (L — Loyal / Loving — Metal — Righteousness)
*Name meaning: lotus, symbolizing resilience.*

Simultaneously fiercely devoted and undeniably intellectual. Unpredictably chaotic. Her quasi-mad-scientist nature stems from peculiar bear fascination so profoundly rooted that she's nearly become one herself. Her Puru assists balancing compulsion against responsible investigation.

**Pair dynamic**: Compatible yet excessively energetic. Loyal heeds Loving's directives regardless of accuracy. Their compassionate inclinations guide them toward morally sound paths despite missteps — embodying Righteousness through trial.

#### Ruan (O — Overstimulated / Overwhelmed — Water — Wisdom)
*Name meaning: CN for soft. Evocative of water, vulnerability.*

Profoundly responsive emotionally, channels energy toward music composition. Traditional and contemporary elements interwoven. Her Puru's companionship taught her how effortlessly she destabilizes others, cultivating greater mindfulness.

**Pair dynamic**: Improbably successful. Overwhelmed struggles with elementary functions, yet Overstimulated maintains focus on achievable particulars. Measured, deliberate, resourceful — embodying Wisdom through methodical effort.

## 6. Jani — The Mascot

### In-World Origin

The **Cave Bera of Tsuheji** — prehistoric cave paintings situated within Old Horai caverns (presently **The Cave of Clay**). These artworks depict a bear-like figure carved into stone, pigmented with red colorants, displaying spiral eyes.

Original caretakers created these paintings pursuing enlightenment through consuming minor quantities of honey leaking from Puruhani vessel fissures. This represented **deep honey** — inadvertently accessing the Puruhani's originating world (OBB realm), depicting imagery from its background.

Throughout history, Cave Bera imagery accumulated cultural significance, enhanced through deep honey memory-intensification, transforming a mere artistic representation into a sentient, interdimensional entity capable of traveling between realms.

### Current Status

Broadly: Jani functions as a cherished metropolitan emblem. An appealing bear adorning merchandise, signage. A commercial brand.

Reality: Jani constitutes an interdimensional consciousness navigating multiple universes. He maintains dialogue with select individuals but otherwise remains enigmatic. **The Puru cult** in Old Horai understands this actuality.

## 9. Key Connections

### OBB → Puruhani → Tsuheji
The Ooga Booga Beras fabricated Puruhani from honey. Puruhani transitioned to Tsuheji, cultivated societal development, subsequently hibernated. Recent excavation has restored them.

### Deep Honey → Cave Paintings → Jani
Early caretakers ingested Puruhani vessel honey, experienced OBB world visions, and rendered the Cave Bera. Accumulated cultural resonance transformed imagery into sentient interdimensional consciousness — Jani.

### Jani → Multiverse
Jani represents singular consciousness expressed distinctly across dimensional boundaries. Within Tsuheji, widespread perception treats him as commercial mascot iconography. The Puru cult recognizes underlying reality.

### HENLO → Everything
The five-character framework (H-E-N-L-O) structures personality pairings, elemental assignments, ethical concepts, chromatic language, and bear classifications.
